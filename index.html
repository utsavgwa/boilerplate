In coding or computer programming, boilerplate code refers to pieces of code that must be included in multiple locations with little or no modification. It is frequently used to describe languages that are deemed verbose, i.e. the programmer must write a lot of code to do simple tasks.
So where does term boilerplate came from ?
In the late 1800s, boilerplate was literally cast or stamped in metal and sent to newspaper presses and businesses across the United States. Thousands of newspapers received and used this type of boilerplate from the nation's largest supplier, the Western Newspaper Union, until the 1950s. Some corporations also handed out boilerplate press releases that had to be printed exactly as prepared.
